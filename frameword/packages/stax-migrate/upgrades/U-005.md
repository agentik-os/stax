# U-005 — Reference rail v2: S-capped, scroll-to-leaf, removable chips, consume-on-resume

TARGET: {{TARGET}}
REFERENCE: frameword/apps/crm-specimen/src/App.tsx (ColumnHost scroll, Panel width cap,
PushHost ref chips, deepLink consume)

## WHY
References are peripheral: full-width refs (esp. L/XL roots) ate the stage, and the
auto-scroll targeted the stage END — with 2+ pins the panel you just opened landed
100% off-screen.

## DETECT
- Auto-scroll to `scrollWidth` (or equivalent) while references render AFTER the path.
- Reference panels rendered at their registry width (L/XL refs).
- Compact ref chips with no remove affordance.
- Resuming a space-root reference leaves a duplicate of the space in the rail.

## APPLY
1. Auto-scroll targets the CONTEXT LEAF's edges into view (rect math), never the rail.
2. Every reference renders capped at S width regardless of registry size.
3. Compact chips: ✶ glyph (no emoji) + a stopPropagation remove ×.
4. Resuming a reference whose target IS a space root consumes the ref after reopening
   (no duplicate); detail refs keep stay-in-rail semantics.
5. Dynamic (store-owned) resources must resolve to full opening paths so their refs
   have clickable titles and can resume.

## VERIFY
- Pin 2 roots, drill in a third space → the new leaf is ≥ 95% visible (rect proof).
- A root-ref resume: rail count decreases by one, space reopens once.
- Compact viewport: chip × removes the reference.
