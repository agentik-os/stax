# U-002 — A×F5 form grammar: the title IS the field; form cards abolished

TARGET: {{TARGET}}
REFERENCE: frameword/apps/crm-specimen — CanvasBoard.tsx (NodeInspector/EdgeInspector),
NotesApp.tsx (TaskDetail), DataApp.tsx (DataRow), Profile.tsx · styles.css (.fs-*, .section)

## WHY
Entity panels (inspectors, tasks, rows, profile) used to wrap their fields in bordered
cards inside the panel — box-in-box. The validated grammar: the entity's identity edits
IN PLACE as the panel's serif title block, and every remaining group is a flat section.

## DETECT
- `grep -rn 'className="card"' src` — any card whose children include `input|textarea|select`
  is a FORM CARD and must be converted. (Editorial/content cards stay.)
- A panel showing BOTH a static title and a "Label" input for the same entity.

## APPLY
1. Title block `.fs-head` (flex column, gap 6, margin-bottom 18):
   - `.fs-title` — serif, `calc(var(--fz-title) * 0.96)`, weight 600, NO border,
     focus = `background: var(--secondary)`, placeholder at 50% opacity.
   - `.fs-sub` — body size, muted, same naked treatment.
   - identity micro-properties (color swatches…) naked under the subtitle, margin-top 12.
2. Every remaining group = `.section`: mono uppercase label (margin-bottom 10) + naked
   content, `border-top: 1px solid var(--border)`, padding 15px 0 16px. NO background,
   NO radius, NO box.
3. The SHELL renders no h2 for these panels — one title, owned by the entity
   (suppress the static panel title for converted panel types).
4. Collection inputs (add a sub-item…) keep the standard thin-border input; list rows
   keep their drill/row style; rich editors keep the smart toolbar with no frame.
5. Vertical rhythm is law: title →6px→ subtitle →12px→ swatches →18px→ first section.

## VERIFY
- `grep -rn 'className="card"' src` — zero remaining form cards (show the output and
  justify every surviving editorial card).
- Screenshot one converted inspector in BOTH themes; measure title→sub gap = 6px.
- No panel shows a duplicated title (static h2 + editable field).
