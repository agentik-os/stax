# U-030: the 100% transfer system — strangler embeds + the parity gate

TARGET: {{TARGET}}
REFERENCE: frameword/apps/crm-specimen (legacy panel) + verify/parity.mjs + M10

## DETECT
- The migration is a rewrite gamble: capabilities exist only after their screen
  is rebuilt, partial states ship, and nothing machine-checks that the rebuilt
  app still does everything the source did.

## APPLY
1. LEGACY EMBED PANELS: panelType "legacy" renders the old screen (iframe src
   or srcDoc) inside the grammar — media-rule border, XL size, a foot note
   naming the replacement plan. Day 1 of adoption, the WHOLE legacy app runs
   inside the Stax shell: navigation, spaces, palette are Stax; every legacy
   screen is one drill away, unchanged. 100% capability before any rebuild.
2. THE PARITY CONTRACT: stax-migration/parity.csv (id, capability, probe
   deep-link hash, expect: panel | action:<id> | text:<needle>) covers EVERY
   source capability. `stax-migrate parity --url` drives each row against the
   live app and exits non-zero unless 100% pass, listing exactly what is lost.
3. REPLACE IN PLACE, GATED: each rebuilt screen swaps its row's probe from the
   embed to the grammar target; parity runs again; less than 100% reverts the
   swap. The contract lives in CI beside the design gate, forever.

## VERIFY
- A 5-row contract over real screens prints "PARITY 100% — 5/5"; adding a row
  for a nonexistent action fails with exit 1 and names it.
- The embed panel renders its legacy content inside a bordered media surface
  with the strangler foot note; the design gate still passes with it open.
