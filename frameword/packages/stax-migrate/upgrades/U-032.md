# U-032: a11y hardening — dark-accent legibility, landmarks, machine-checked contrast

TARGET: {{TARGET}}
REFERENCE: frameword/apps/crm-specimen (tokens.css dark block, <main> stage, e2e suite)

## DETECT
- The dark theme keeps a light/white --accent-foreground on a BRIGHT dark-mode
  accent (dark accents are usually raised to L≈0.7 for visibility): white on a
  bright fill measures ~2.5:1 — an illegible primary CTA.
- No <main> landmark: the panel stage is an anonymous div, so screen readers
  have no jump target for the workspace.
- Contrast is asserted by eye, never measured: oklch computed styles defeat
  naive rgb parsing, so nobody checks.

## APPLY
1. DARK-ACCENT LEGIBILITY LAW: the accent's foreground follows the accent's
   LIGHTNESS, per theme. Light theme: dark/deep accent (L≈0.55) carries white
   text. Dark theme: the accent brightens (L≈0.7) so its text goes NEAR-BLACK
   (oklch ~0.145) — never keep white by inertia. Applies to every accent-filled
   control: primary CTA, destructive, record/live buttons, step markers,
   composer send.
2. LANDMARKS: the stage renders as <main aria-label="Workspace stage">; the
   sidebar is <aside>, topbar nav is <nav>; the toast region keeps aria-live.
3. MEASURED CONTRAST: assert ratios in the test rig, converting computed
   oklch through a 1x1 canvas readback (fillStyle + getImageData → sRGB),
   then WCAG: (L1+0.05)/(L2+0.05) ≥ 4.5 for body-size text, 3.0 ≥ 18.7px.
   Muted inks on card/secondary and accent foregrounds are the required pairs,
   in BOTH themes.

## VERIFY
- .foot-cta contrast ≥ 4.5:1 in light AND dark (canvas-converted).
- document.querySelector("main") exists; the drill list is reachable by Tab
  and the first Tab lands on a control with a visible outline.
- Muted mono labels (eyebrow, stat lab, crumb, foot-note) ≥ 4.5:1 both themes.
